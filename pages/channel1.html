Exhibiting Technology: The Television

Roan Fijen

This digital exibit explores the role of the television in the United States during the twentieth century, not as a technological innovation but as a force that changed how Americans communicated and spent their time. Rather than a chronological story of "firsts", this exhibit will examine the television from two different perspectives: its influence on daily life and its role in American politics.

The first section, "Television and American Daily Life", approaches television from a domestic and social perspective. This section focuses on how television changed the way Americans spent their time, interacted with one another, and engaged with entertainment.

The second section, "Television and American Politics", examines television as a tool of political communication and influence. This section focuses on how television changed the way political campaigns were run and how voters perceived their candidates. Rather than simply relaying information, the television introduced a visual aspect to politics, where the appearance and charisma of political candidates became increasingly important.
